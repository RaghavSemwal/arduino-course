# Notes - Tutorial 2: Understanding How LEDs Work

🔬 What an LED is
A p–n junction diode made from semiconductor materials like gallium arsenide (GaAs) or gallium nitride (GaN).

When current flows forward, electrons from the n‑side and holes from the p‑side meet in the junction.

Their recombination releases photons instead of heat — unlike incandescent bulbs that rely on heating a filament

🌈 Why LEDs emit different colors
The band gap of the semiconductor determines the photon energy → which determines the light color.

Larger band gap → higher‑energy photons → blue/UV

Smaller band gap → lower‑energy photons → red/IR 


⚡ Step‑by‑step: How an LED produces light
Forward bias applied  
Voltage pushes electrons toward the p‑region and holes toward the n‑region.

Carrier injection  
Electrons and holes enter the depletion region.

Recombination  
They meet and recombine. Instead of producing heat, the energy is released as photons (electroluminescence).

Photon emission  
The LED’s shape and coatings help photons escape efficiently because semiconductors have a high refractive index

💡 Why LEDs are so efficient
No filament → no thermal losses.

Direct electrical-to-light conversion → high efficiency.

Long lifespan due to solid‑state construction.
